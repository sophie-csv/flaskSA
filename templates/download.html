Basic Sentiment Analysis

What is aspect-based sentiment analysis?

Basic sentiment analysis is the process of determining the emotional tone or sentiment expressed in a piece of text. It typically classifies the text into categories such as positive, negative, or neutral, based on the words or phrases used. This analysis helps to understand the overall sentiment conveyed in customer reviews, social media posts, or other written content.

Positive Sentiment WordCloud

What does this mean?

A simple general positive word cloud displays positive words like "happy" or "great," with larger words appearing more frequently. The size of the words indicates how often they appear, giving a quick visual of the overall positive sentiment.

Negative Sentiment WordCloud

What does this mean?

A simple general negative word cloud shows negative words like "sad" or "bad," with larger words appearing more frequently. The size of the words reflects their frequency, providing a quick visual of the overall negative sentiment.

Positive Frequency Graph

What does this mean?

A Positive Frequency Graph shows how often positive words or sentiments appear in a dataset. The x-axis represents individual positive words or time intervals, while the y-axis indicates the frequency of each word or sentiment. This graph visually represents the distribution and intensity of positive expressions throughout the data.

Negative Frequency Graph

What does this mean?

A Negative Frequency Graph shows how often negative words or sentiments appear in a dataset. The x-axis represents individual negative words or time intervals, while the y-axis indicates the frequency of each word or sentiment. This graph visually represents the distribution and intensity of negative expressions throughout the data.
